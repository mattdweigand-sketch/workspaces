## How We Communicate
[Describe the relationship in the organization's writing across audiences (customers, partners,
internal teams, the public). Expert peers or first-time users? Warm and plainspoken or formal and
precise? How direct is the organization about bad news and uncertainty? This applies everywhere.]

## How We Sound
[3-5 patterns in the organization's writing — not adjectives ("clear," "confident"), actual
patterns. "States the answer in the first sentence." "Names the problem directly rather than
softening it into 'challenges.'" "Explains an issue before the reader has to ask, paired with the
action being taken." "Takes a clear position; does not hedge with 'it could be argued that.'"]

## How We Handle Bad News
[The organization's approach to delivering a miss, a delay, an outage, or uncertainty. Lead with
it or contextualize first? Pair every problem with the action being taken and a timeline? This is
where trust is built or lost, and the instinct is the same whether it is a customer email or an
internal incident note. Be specific.]

## What Our Voice Is NOT
[The boundaries that catch drift, everywhere the organization writes. "Not promotional (no
'incredible,' 'thrilled,' 'game-changing,' no exclamation points)." "Not hedging mush
('approximately 14%')." "Not falsely reassuring." "Not buried in throat-clearing before the
point." These negative rules eliminate the most common failure directions.]

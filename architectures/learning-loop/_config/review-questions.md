# Canonical Review Questions

## The Questions
[A fixed list the analysis answers for every process, won or lost. Write 8-15
specific questions. The set below is the recommended starting bank — adapt to
your team:

1. How did this opportunity reach us, and through which counterparty/intermediary
   or direct path?
2. What was our thesis, and the terms/structure we offered?
3. What was the winning offer / outcome, and our gap to it — in the key figure
   *and* in terms?
4. On what dimension was it won or lost: price, certainty/speed, structure,
   terms, reputation/relationship, or timing?
5. Counterparty's *stated* reason vs. our *assessed* real reason? (The spin-defense —
   keep the two distinct and say which you believe.)
6. Did we compete with full information, or blind on a key term?
7. Where in the process did we gain or lose ground (first round, final round,
   last look)?
8. Did our reputation or track record help or hurt?
9. If lost: what realistically would have changed it — and at what point would it
   have stopped being a case we wanted?
10. If won: what was the decisive edge, and what nearly lost it?
11. What would we do differently in a similar process (a relationship to build, a
    term to pre-clear, a faster path to certainty)?]

## Answering Rules
[How to answer for comparability:
- Answer every question, in order. Use "unknown" where the record does not
  support an answer — never fabricate to fill the slot.
- Keep answers tight and factual; the why and the lessons have their own
  sections in the analysis output.
- Distinguish the counterparty's stated reason from your assessed reason explicitly,
  and never collapse them into one.]

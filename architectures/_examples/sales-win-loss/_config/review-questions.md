# Canonical Review Questions — Tessera sales win/loss review

The fixed question set every win/loss review answers, in the same order, every time. This is the
single most important file in the workspace: it is what makes records comparable, and
comparability is what lets the store reveal patterns. L3 reference, loaded in stage 02.

Question 5 (stated vs. assessed reason) is load-bearing. The rep's stated reason for a loss is
often the comfortable one — "price" or "they picked a competitor" — not the real one. Forcing the
stated and assessed reasons apart on every record is the defense against the store filling up with
self-serving explanations that send the next rep down the wrong path.

Keep the set stable. Changing the questions breaks comparability with every record captured under
the old set.

## The Questions

1. How did this opportunity reach us (inbound, outbound, partner, expansion), and what was the
   ARR and the segment?
2. What was the pipeline path — the stages it moved through, how long the cycle ran, and where it
   stalled or advanced?
3. Who did we engage, and how deep was the threading — single-threaded, lightly multi-threaded,
   or multi-threaded with the economic buyer reached? Name the champion and the economic buyer if
   known.
4. What did the buyer say they were solving for, and against what alternative (a named competitor,
   an in-house build, or the status quo)?
5. **The rep's *stated* reason for the outcome vs. the *assessed* real reason?** (The spin-defense
   — keep the two distinct and say which the evidence supports.) Ask "why" past the first answer:
   if "price," was a discount ever requested, and did anyone with budget authority say no?
6. What was the reason class (the mechanism), distinct from the rep's framing?
7. Where in the cycle did momentum break or hold (discovery, technical evaluation, security
   review, procurement, final decision), and what slowed or sped it?
8. What made this outcome worse — or better — than it had to be (a missing relationship, an
   unaddressed objection, a security review left to the end, an early multi-thread)?
9. If lost: what realistically would have changed it — and at what point would it have stopped
   being winnable?
10. If won: what was the decisive edge, and what nearly lost it?
11. What is the transferable play — what should the next rep do differently in a similar
    opportunity (a stakeholder to reach early, an objection to pre-empt, a security review to
    front-load)?

## Answering Rules

- Answer every question, in order. Use "unknown" where the record does not support an answer —
  never fabricate to fill a slot.
- Keep answers tight and factual; the why and the transferable play have their own sections in the
  analysis output.
- Distinguish the rep's stated reason from the assessed reason explicitly, and never collapse them.

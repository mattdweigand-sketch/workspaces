## ARR / Outcome
$48K ARR annual contract. Closed-Lost to Conduit, a competing integration vendor.

## Canonical Answers (summary)
Inbound demo request from a data analyst, who ran the whole evaluation. Solid technical fit and a
positive POC. The analyst liked Tessera but had no budget authority and no executive sponsor was
ever brought in. The evaluation drifted past Bluepeak's budget cycle (Q1 close); by the time a
decision was needed, the analyst's manager picked Conduit, which had been working a parallel
relationship with the head of IT. Tessera never reached that economic buyer.

## Stated vs. Assessed Reason
*Stated* (rep, close note): "went with a competitor — Conduit had a better relationship and we lost
the bake-off."
*Assessed:* the real driver was **no executive sponsor and a single-threaded evaluation that drifted
past the budget window**. Conduit won not on product but because it had multi-threaded to the head
of IT (the economic buyer) while Tessera stayed with one analyst. "Went with a competitor" describes
the outcome, not the cause — we lost access to the decision, not a feature comparison. The two
diverged. [assessed HIGH]

## Validated Reason
`champion-loss` / no-EB-access mechanism, surfacing as a competitor loss: single-threaded on a
no-authority analyst, no executive sponsor, evaluation drifted past the budget cycle, and the
competitor reached the economic buyer first. Conduit relationship = trigger; our threading gap =
cause. [HIGH]

## Transferable Play
- Identify and reach the economic buyer early; do not let an inbound evaluation run entirely
  through a single no-authority contact.
- Map the budget cycle at discovery and pace the evaluation to land inside it.

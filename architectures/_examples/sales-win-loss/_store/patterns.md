# Tessera Win/Loss Patterns

Rolled-up intelligence across the opportunity records. This is the file the next rep, and sales
enablement / pipeline strategy, read first. A pattern is "stated" at 3+ supporting records; below
that it is "emerging." Records are append-only; patterns are revised in place with dated notes.

Records in store: 3 (Atlas Freight — lost; Bluepeak Retail — lost; Cedar Health — won).

---

## STATED PATTERNS (3+ records)

### P1 — Reaching (or failing to reach) the economic buyer decides the outcome; "price" and "competitor" are stated reasons but decisive in 0 of 2 losses
**Segments:** threading depth and economic-buyer access; spans ARR bands (Mid and Large) and
verticals (Logistics, Retail, Healthcare).
**Evidence:** 3 records.
- Atlas Freight (lost, $84K): single-threaded on one champion, no EB ever reached; champion
  departed and the opportunity died. Rep stated "price" — but no discount was ever requested and
  no budget holder was ever in the opportunity.
- Bluepeak Retail (lost, $48K): single-threaded on a no-authority analyst, no executive sponsor;
  evaluation drifted past the budget cycle and the competitor reached the EB first. Rep stated
  "went with a competitor" — but the assessed driver was the EB-access gap.
- Cedar Health (won, $96K): multi-threaded to the economic buyer by stage 2, security cleared
  early; won over a comparable competitor on access and a de-risked path.
**Confidence:** High. The same factor — economic-buyer access — sinks both losses and wins the one
win. In both losses the stated reason ("price," "competitor") was decisive in neither; the assessed
driver was the threading gap every time.
**Last updated:** 2026-05-12 (Atlas Freight added; promoted from emerging to stated).
**Action:** Make economic-buyer access a stage-gate on opportunities above the Mid ARR band — do
not advance a Large opportunity past technical evaluation while it is single-threaded. Multi-thread
to the EB by stage 2 as the default motion, not a recovery move.

---

## EMERGING PATTERNS (<3 records)

### P2 — Leaving the security review to the end lets it kill a deal that has lost its sponsor; front-loading it removes the risk
**Segments:** procurement / security stage.
**Evidence:** 2 records (Atlas Freight, Cedar Health).
- Atlas Freight: security questionnaire arrived late and stalled with no internal owner — it
  closed off recovery once the champion was gone.
- Cedar Health: the SOC 2 package was sent in week two, before the technical evaluation finished;
  security never became a blocker and the cleared path defused a late price question.
**Confidence:** Low-medium — 2 of 3, and entangled with P1 (in Atlas the security stall only
mattered because there was no sponsor left). Worth watching as a distinct lever.
**Last updated:** 2026-05-12.
**Watch:** the next opportunity where we front-load security but stay single-threaded — does early
security alone save it, or is EB access (P1) the thing that actually matters? That record separates
P2 from P1.

- None outright contradict yet. Open question on P1: is the EB-access gap a *coaching* problem
  (reps default to single-threading) or a *process* problem (no stage-gate forces multi-threading)?
  Both losses suggest the latter, which is why the fix is a stage-gate, not just training — but a
  record where a rep multi-threads without a gate and still wins would tell us coaching is enough.

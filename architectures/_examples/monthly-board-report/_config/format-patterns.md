# Format Patterns — Northwind Analytics board reporting

Structure for each report type. L3 reference, loaded in stage 02. The draft stage reads the
section matching what it is producing.

## Monthly Board Report

1. **Opening summary** — one short paragraph. The headline metric (ARR), the single most
   important development of the month, and the one thing the board should worry about.
2. **Metrics** — the standing dashboard the board expects every month, as a short table: ARR,
   net revenue retention, gross margin, monthly burn, cash, runway, headcount. Each with the
   month-over-month change.
3. **Commentary** — two to four short paragraphs. What moved and why: growth, retention, burn.
   Address the single biggest board concern head-on.
4. **Risks and watch items** — the things that could go wrong, each with what is being done.
5. **Asks of the board** — concrete decisions or help requested, or "none this month."
6. **Disclosures** — the required confidentiality and forward-looking footer (see
   `constraints.md`).

Length: two to three pages. The board reads it all; do not pad.

## Flash / Mid-Month Update

Tighter. ARR and cash only, plus anything that changed materially since the last board report.
No commentary beyond the exception.

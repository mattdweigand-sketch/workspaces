To the Board,

ARR reached $14.4 million in May, up $0.5 million (3.6%) and the strongest net-new month of the
year. The driver was two enterprise expansions. Cash discipline held: net burn was $0.62 million,
in plan, and runway sits at 13 months. The one thing to watch is that runway is now inside the
window where we should begin framing the next raise.

## Commentary

**Growth.** Net new ARR was $0.50 million ($0.71 million booked, $0.21 million churned), our best
month this year. Two enterprise expansions accounted for $0.34 million of new bookings, which
tells us the land-and-expand motion is working in the enterprise segment.

**Retention.** Net revenue retention rose a point to 112%. May churn of $0.21 million was within
the normal band, and the largest single piece was a mid-market plan downgrade of $0.09 million,
not a logo loss. No churn pattern to flag.

**Burn and cash.** Net burn rose $0.04 million to $0.62 million, from three new hires and annual
software renewals that landed in May. Cash fell by exactly that month's burn, with no financing
or one-time items. Margin held at 78%.

## Risks and Watch Items

- **Runway.** At 13 months of runway on the current burn, we should begin raise preparation now
  rather than under time pressure. Finance is building the materials this quarter.
- **Enterprise concentration.** Growth is increasingly enterprise-led, which is good for NRR but
  raises single-account exposure. We are tracking top-account concentration monthly.

## Asks of the Board

- Input on raise timing and target size, given the runway position. We will bring a fuller plan
  next month.

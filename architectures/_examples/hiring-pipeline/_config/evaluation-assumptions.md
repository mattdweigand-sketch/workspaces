# Screen Assumptions — Senior Backend Engineer

The standard assumptions the evaluate stage uses for the early read, before the full interview
loop. The point is consistency: every candidate gets the same rough treatment at the screen, so
early reads are comparable. These are deliberately coarse.

CRITICAL: the screen is a filter, not the hire decision. A screen read catches obvious
non-starters and routes the promising candidates into the full loop. The full loop — four
structured interviews against the scorecard — is the real evaluation. Never let a strong screen
read substitute for the loop.

## As-Of Date

2026-04-01. Refresh when the role's must-have stack or the comp band moves.

## Standard Screen Read (coarse, by signal)

- **Resume + recruiter screen** establish: years at the senior bar, languages/stack match,
  scope of past systems (request volume, data scale, team size led on a design).
- A senior candidate should show **at least one system they designed and operated**, not just
  contributed to. Absence of any ownership story is a screen-level yellow flag, not an automatic
  decline.
- Stack match is directional at the screen: strong Go/Java is a plus; a strong distributed-
  systems background in another typed language clears the screen and gets tested in the loop.

## Resourcing Assumptions

- A full loop costs roughly 5 interviewer-hours plus 4 candidate-hours. The screen exists so the
  team spends those hours only on candidates who can plausibly clear the bar.
- Standard loop = recruiter screen, hiring-manager screen, then a four-panel onsite (coding,
  system design, collaboration/values, ownership/past-projects).

## Screen Bar

A candidate clears the screen if they plausibly meet the senior level on stack and scope and
show at least one ownership story. Clearing the screen means "worth a full loop," not "likely to
get an offer."

## Use Notes

- Label every screen-stage read as a screen estimate, not a scorecard rating. Scorecard ratings
  (Strong / Mixed / Weak) come only from the full loop.
- Do not refine these assumptions per candidate. If a candidate needs special-case reasoning to
  clear the screen, that is a loop question — advance and test it, or decline.

# Decision Criteria — Senior Backend Engineer, Northwind Analytics

What the team is hiring for. This is the standard the evaluate stage measures every candidate
against. L3 reference, stable across candidates (it changes when the role changes, not per
applicant).

## Role / Scope

Senior Backend Engineer on the Platform team. Owns service design and delivery for the data
ingestion and API layer. Expected to lead the technical design of medium-to-large features,
mentor two to three mid-level engineers, and carry on-call rotation. Not a management role; this
is a senior individual contributor.

## Domain / Coverage

Backend services in a typed language (Go or Java preferred; strong Python considered).
Distributed systems, relational and time-series data stores, queue-based pipelines, public REST
APIs. Not in scope for this role: front-end ownership, data science, infrastructure/SRE as a
primary focus (we have a separate platform-reliability team).

## Level / Bar

Senior = independent on ambiguous problems, sets the technical direction for a feature area, and
raises the bar for the engineers around them. Below that is a Mid hire (different requisition);
above that, where the candidate is operating as a de facto staff engineer, is a different
scorecard and a different compensation band.

## Scorecard (the four dimensions every candidate is rated on)

1. **Technical depth** — designs sound distributed systems; reasons about failure modes,
   consistency, and scale; writes production-grade code.
2. **System design** — decomposes an ambiguous problem into services and contracts; makes and
   defends tradeoffs.
3. **Collaboration / mentorship** — raises the team; communicates a design clearly; gives and
   takes review well.
4. **Ownership / delivery** — ships, follows through on operability, owns incidents.

Each dimension is rated Strong / Mixed / Weak by the interview loop.

## Target Profile

A candidate who is at least **Strong on Technical depth and System design** (the two core
dimensions for this role) and **no worse than Mixed** on the other two, with no automatic
disqualifier. That is the bar an offer must clear.
